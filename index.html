About me

I am a postdoctoral research fellow at Gordon Center for Medical Imaging, Massachusetts General Hospital and Harvard Medical School, advised by Kuang Gong, Ph.D.. Now, I am doing research about medical image processing by deep learning techniques, especially for positron emission tomography (PET) image processing. My research projects include medical image synthesis and segmentation. Previously, I received my Ph.D. degree from Zhejiang University, advised by Prof. Yanpeng Cao. During Ph.D. time, my research focused on signal and image processing in infrared non-destructive testing. My work includes pre-processing (image quality enhancement), sub-surface defect detection, sub-surface defect depth estimation and spatial visualization of defect.
